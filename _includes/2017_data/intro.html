Background

The Eighteenth Meeting of the APEC Alliance for Supply Chain Connectivity (A2C2) will be held on August 25, 2026, in Dalian, China. Established to advance supply chain performance across the Asia-Pacific region, the A2C2 brings together government and private sector stakeholders to identify practical solutions to supply chain challenges and strengthen regional connectivity. This year's meeting builds on discussions from the 2026 A2C2 Regional Roundtable in Mexico City and continues efforts to promote public-private and cross-fora collaboration on trade and supply chain issues.

Focus

This year's meeting will examine the impact of pre-arrival processing requirements on supply chain efficiency and trade facilitation. By enabling customs authorities to review information before goods arrive, pre-arrival processing can reduce border delays, improve predictability for traders, lower transaction costs, and support more resilient regional supply chains. The meeting will also feature updates from leaders of other APEC groups on cross-cutting initiatives and emerging priorities affecting regional connectivity.

Audience

The meeting is intended for APEC customs and trade officials, representatives from the Committee on Trade and Investment (CTI) and Sub-Committee on Customs Procedures (SCCP), private sector stakeholders, logistics and supply chain professionals, and other experts working to strengthen trade facilitation and regional supply chain connectivity.
